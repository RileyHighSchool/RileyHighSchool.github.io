Hear from Students

What our amazing students have to say about the magnet programs.

Margo Bokhart

Class of 2016

When I think back to high school, the engineering program was a key part. It was always one of my favorite classes to go to. The program really helped me open my eyes and think about things in abstract ways. To this day I still look at something and think “how can I make that better” and I’d have to thank the Riley engineering program for that.

Sharon Njeri

Class of 2015

I really like the diversity at Riley. It was good to work with so many different people. As a woman, I could work with all these different people, which helped me translate that to college. I was prepared at Riley for Purdue.

Jadon Grundy

Class of 2015

You will learn many new and interesting things. You will also learn how to stay on task and be consistent, as well as working with others. The program is very hands on and uses high end computer software.

Allison Walker

Class of 2017

Several of my PLTW classes prepared me for classes in college. I really enjoyed learning how to 3D model in high school and how that put me at an advantage in college. My most memorable class from PLTW was the senior year engineering project. I really enjoyed seeing a year of work come to action and design a solution to a problem I was tasked to solve. In college I had several design classes similar to the senior year class and thought it was neat to follow the same design process I learned in high school.

Mila Medich

Class of 2017

I am so thankful for the engineering program at Riley because it gave me such a strong community within the field and it showed me how I don’t need to change myself to fit into engineering but instead I could change engineering to fit to me and that led me to find my own path within the realm of engineering!

Ricardo Carbajal-Diaz

Class of 2017

Develop a habit of studying. Get involved in something that makes you stand out. Almost everyone you meet in college was in their school’s top 10% and had the 4.0 GPA, differentiate yourself.

Isaac Walters

Class of 2019

The Riley magnet really helped me learn how to problem solve. It also helped me know how to read prints because I have to do that on a day to day basis. I also use the electrical formula to find amps off of the voltage. So even with not going to college to be an engineer the Riley magnet really helped me with my trade job.

Jane Kimani

Class of 2019

The program played a huge role in my decision to pursue a degree in the STEM field, because I was able to gain hands on experience in the engineering and technology field and explore different career fields within STEM through the program. Riley has THE best teachers in South Bend!

Braeden Allison

Class of 2020

The environment at Riley High school was very beneficial to me by surrounding me with a diverse group of students coming from very different walks of life. It helped prepare me socially for the world outside of high school and I’ve always appreciated that about my Riley experience.

Matthew Williams II

Class of 2022

Find a really solid group of friends you can depend on and trust. Also know that the first group you find at the beginning might not be the same people you hang out with later on down the line. Also if you have a passion and you wanna make it your career go for it, just know it might be thought and difficult to get to where you want to be. It'll take a lot of hard work but it will be worth it and the journey can be quite fun and interesting at times. It will have its challenges though.
